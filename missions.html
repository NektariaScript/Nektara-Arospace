Mission Log

Every journey begins with a question left unanswered.

A record of where we've been, where we are, and where curiosity is asking us to go next — organized not by distance, but by the questions each mission tries to answer.

Completed

What we've already learned

Missions that reached their objective and came home with answers — and a fresh set of questions.

2014

Marrow Probe

Nektara's first deep-space instrument, sent to study the internal density variations of a near-Earth asteroid. Established the seismic modeling techniques still used across the fleet today.

Complete
2017

Quietfield Balloon Array

A network of high-altitude balloons that spent eleven months listening for atmospheric gravity waves above the poles, quietly gathering data no ground station could reach.

Complete
2019

Halcyon Orbiter

Mapped the mineral composition of the lunar south pole, completing its survey and returning the most detailed shadow-region atlas ever assembled by a private agency.

Complete
2023

Sable Comet Flyby

A single close pass by a long-period comet, capturing spectral readings of its tail composition as it approached the inner solar system for the first time in nine centuries.

Complete

2025

Cinder Rover, Second Traverse

Our surface rover is midway through a slow crossing of a volcanic plain, sampling rock strata that may hold clues to the planet's early atmosphere.

In progress

Vesper Deep Listening Array

A constellation of quiet-orbit radio telescopes currently tuning into the faint murmur of the early universe, far from Earth's electromagnetic noise.

In progress

Lantern Relay Constellation

A growing network of small communication satellites built to keep every Nektara mission — present and future — in constant, gentle contact with home.

What curiosity is asking of us next

Early-stage concepts, still being shaped in the workshop, the questions we hope to be equipped to ask within the decade.

2029

Threnody Ice-Moon Lander

A proposed lander for an icy moon in the outer solar system, designed to drill beneath the surface and search for the chemical signatures of a subsurface ocean.

Proposed
2031

Aphelion Voyage

A proposed crewed journey to a slow, stable orbit beyond the asteroid belt, designed to study what long solitude in deep space does to the human mind.

Proposed
2034

Farlight Interstellar Probe

A concept for a lightweight probe designed to leave the solar system entirely, carrying nothing but instruments, a message, and a great deal of patience.
